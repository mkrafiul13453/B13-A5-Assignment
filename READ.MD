1.Answer:
var: It is function-scoped and can be re-declared and updated. It was used in older JavaScript versions and may cause unexpected bugs.
let: It is block-scoped and can be updated but cannot be re-declared in the same scope. You can change the value. 
const: It is also block-scoped but cannot be updated or re-declared after initialization. It means the value is constant.
